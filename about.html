1 The story

How a dive shop
became an institution.

Most businesses don't make it four years. Tom's has made it four decades — by doing two things stubbornly well: teaching people to be safe in the water, and treating every customer like they'll be back next week. Because they usually are.

1982

The doors open

Tom's opens on Burnet Road as a scuba shop with a simple idea: Austin deserves a real dive shop with real training, not a counter that sells gear and waves goodbye. The indoor pool follows — warm, small, and built for teaching.

1990s

The swim school grows up

What started as scuba pool sessions becomes a full swim school. Austin parents discover the warmest teaching pool in town, and the family business gains its second family: swimmers who start at six months old.

2004

Travel takes off

Group trips become a fixture — Cozumel, Bonaire, Roatán, and beyond. Tom's instructors lead them all, and the shop becomes the place Austin divers plan their year around.

2013

The Aquanauts form

A few regulars who were tired of coordinating dives by group text start the Austin Aquanauts, the city's original dive club, headquartered at Tom's. Today it's 200+ members strong.

Today

Same block. Same mission.

New ownership, same family values, same warm pool. Tom's continues as Austin's home for everything water — now teaching the grandchildren of its first students.

2 The team

70+ people who'd rather
be underwater.

Dive instructors, swim coaches, gear technicians, and front-counter folks who can answer almost any question about water. Many started as Tom's students. Some never left.

Dive instruction

The instructors

SSI-certified professionals teaching everything from first bubbles to Instructor ratings. Between them: thousands of certifications, every ocean, and an unreasonable number of dive stories.

Swim school

The coaches

Patient, certified swim instructors trained in the SSI curriculum. They know that teaching a scared four-year-old to float is harder — and more important — than any flip turn.

Shop & service

3 What we believe

Three things we've never changed.

i

Safety is the product

Everything we sell — lessons, certs, gear, fills — is in service of people being safe and confident in the water.

ii

Small classes, real attention

Four kids per swim class. Small dive courses. We've never optimized for volume and we're not starting now.

iii

Community over customers

People come for a lesson and stay for decades. That only happens when a shop is something more than a shop.

Come see it for yourself.

The best way to understand Tom's is to walk in. Smell the neoprene, hear the splashing, watch a kid swim their first lap. We're on Burnet Road, same as always.
